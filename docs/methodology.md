# Data Analytics Project Methodology (DAPM)

## Purpose

The Data Analytics Project Methodology (DAPM) provides a structured, repeatable, and business-first approach to executing end-to-end data analytics projects.

The methodology emphasizes understanding the business before exploring the data, generating evidence before drawing conclusions, and delivering actionable recommendations that support business decision-making.

# Phase 1 — Business Understanding

## Objective

Develop a clear understanding of the business domain, processes, objectives, and challenges before beginning technical work.

### Deliverable

Business Understanding Document

---

# Phase 2 — Stakeholder Analysis

## Objective

Identify the stakeholders involved in the project and understand the decisions they need to make.

### Deliverable

Stakeholder Analysis Document

---

# Phase 3 — Business Requirements Gathering

## Objective

Gather the business questions, metrics, KPIs, and reporting requirements that define the project's success.

### Deliverable

Business Requirements Document

---

# Phase 4 — Data Discovery & Collection

## Objective

Identify, collect, and organize all relevant data required for the analysis.

### Deliverable

Data Inventory

Collected Datasets

---

# Phase 5 — Data Validation

## Objective

Evaluate the quality of the collected data before performing any analysis.

Typical validation activities include:

- Checking missing values
- Detecting duplicate records
- Verifying data types
- Identifying invalid values
- Assessing data consistency

### Deliverable

Validation Report

---

# Phase 6 — Data Cleaning & Preparation

## Objective

Prepare an analysis-ready dataset by correcting issues identified during validation.

Typical preparation activities include:

- Handling missing values
- Removing duplicates
- Standardizing formats
- Transforming data types
- Integrating multiple datasets

### Deliverable

Clean Analytical Dataset

---

# Phase 7 — Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## Objective

Explore the dataset to understand patterns, trends, distributions, relationships, and anomalies.

EDA consists of three activities.

### Analysis

Perform exploratory calculations and summaries to understand the dataset.

### Visualization

Use visualizations to better understand patterns and relationships.

### Observations

Document objective findings supported directly by the data.

### Deliverable

EDA Report

---

# Phase 8 — Business Insights

## Objective

Interpret EDA observations within the business context to answer stakeholder questions.

Business insights should connect analytical findings with business operations and decision-making.

### Deliverable

Business Insights Report

---

# Phase 9 — Recommendations

## Objective

Develop practical, evidence-based recommendations supported by business insights.

Recommendations should provide clear actions that stakeholders can implement.

### Deliverable

Recommendation Report

---

# Phase 10 — Reporting

## Objective

Communicate the complete analytical journey and business findings in a clear and professional manner.

Reporting may include:

- Dashboards
- Executive summaries
- Business reports
- Presentations
- Portfolio documentation

### Deliverable

Final Business Report

---

# Methodology Principles

Throughout every phase of DAPM:

- Business understanding precedes technical implementation.
- Every phase produces a unique deliverable.
- Documentation evolves throughout the project lifecycle.
- Decisions should be supported by evidence.
- Recommendations should create measurable business value.

---

# Methodology Evolution

DAPM is an evolving methodology.

Each completed case study should be reviewed to identify opportunities for improving the framework, refining terminology, and enhancing project templates.
