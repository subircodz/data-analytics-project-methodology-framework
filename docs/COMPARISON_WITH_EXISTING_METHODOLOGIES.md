> This document provides a high-level comparison between the **DAPM Framework (Data Analytics Project Methodology)** and several widely recognized data analytics methodologies.
>
> The purpose of this document is **not** to claim superiority over existing methodologies, but to clarify the design philosophy of DAPM and explain where it aligns with or differs from established approaches.
>

---

# Why DAPM?

Over the years, numerous methodologies have been developed to standardize data analytics, data mining, and machine learning projects.

Some focus primarily on technical workflows.

Others emphasize knowledge discovery or predictive modeling.

During the development of DAPM, the primary objective was different.

The motivation behind DAPM was to create a **business-first methodology** that helps analysts approach real-world business problems in a structured, evidence-driven manner before beginning technical analysis.

Rather than replacing existing methodologies, DAPM aims to complement them by emphasizing business understanding, stakeholder engagement, and practical decision-making throughout an analytics project.

# Design Philosophy

## CRISP-DM

CRISP-DM is one of the most widely adopted methodologies for data mining projects.

It provides a structured lifecycle beginning with business understanding and progressing through data preparation, modeling, evaluation, and deployment.

DAPM shares several principles with CRISP-DM, particularly the importance of understanding business objectives before technical work begins.

However, DAPM places additional emphasis on stakeholder analysis, business requirements gathering, and evidence-based consulting deliverables.

---

## KDD

KDD (Knowledge Discovery in Databases) focuses primarily on extracting useful knowledge from large datasets.

Its workflow is strongly centered around data processing, transformation, pattern discovery, and interpretation.

DAPM uses many analytical principles similar to KDD but expands the project lifecycle to include consulting-oriented activities before data analysis begins.

---

## SEMMA

SEMMA was developed by SAS as a methodology for statistical analysis and predictive modeling.

It emphasizes:

- Sampling
- Exploring
- Modifying
- Modeling
- Assessing

SEMMA is highly effective for analytical modeling but assumes that business objectives have already been established.

DAPM introduces several project phases before technical analysis to better support business consulting engagements.

---

# What Makes DAPM Different?

DAPM was designed around one guiding principle:

> **Business First. Evidence Always.**

The framework intentionally separates activities that are often combined in practice, including:

- Business Understanding
- Stakeholder Analysis
- Business Requirements
- Data Discovery
- Data Validation
- Data Cleaning & Preparation
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Business Insights
- Recommendations
- Executive Summary

This separation encourages analysts to distinguish between business assumptions, validated evidence, analytical findings, and final recommendations.

---

# Positioning of DAPM

DAPM should not be viewed as a replacement for established methodologies.

Instead, it should be considered a complementary business analytics workflow that emphasizes:

- Structured business engagement
- Stakeholder-driven analysis
- Evidence-based decision making
- Clear consulting deliverables
- Practical project documentation

The framework is intended primarily for business analytics projects rather than specialized machine learning or data mining workflows.
