# DAPM Glossary

This glossary defines the terminology used throughout the Data Analytics Project Methodology (DAPM).

The objective is to establish consistent language across projects, templates, documentation, and reports.

---

# Business Problem

A challenge, opportunity, or objective that the organization wants to address through data analysis.

---

# Stakeholder

A person or group that uses the results of the analysis to support business decisions.

---

# Business Requirement

Information required by stakeholders to answer business questions and support decision-making.

---

# Metric

A measurable value used to monitor business performance.

Example:

- Monthly Revenue
- Orders Delivered
- Inventory Level

---

# Key Performance Indicator (KPI)

A metric that directly measures progress toward a strategic business objective.

Every KPI is a metric.

Not every metric is a KPI.

---

# Data Discovery

The process of identifying, locating, collecting, and understanding available data sources.

---

# Data Validation

The process of assessing the quality, completeness, consistency, and reliability of collected data before analysis.

---

# Data Cleaning

The process of correcting or removing data quality issues identified during validation.

---

# Data Preparation

The process of transforming cleaned data into an analysis-ready dataset.

---

# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

The process of exploring prepared data to identify patterns, relationships, trends, distributions, and anomalies.

EDA describes what the data shows.

---

# Observation

A factual statement supported directly by the data.

Observations should not explain why something happened.

---

# Business Insight

An interpretation of one or more observations within the business context.

Insights explain what the observations mean for the organization.

---

# Recommendation

A proposed action developed from business insights to improve business outcomes.

Recommendations should be practical, evidence-based, and aligned with stakeholder objectives.

---

# Visualization

A graphical representation of data used to support analysis and communicate findings.

Visualizations support observations but are not conclusions.

---

# Evidence

The data, calculations, and visualizations that support observations, insights, and recommendations.

---

# Traceability

The ability to trace every recommendation back to supporting business insights, observations, and underlying data.

---

# Executive Summary

A concise business-focused summary highlighting the problem, findings, insights, recommendations, and expected business value.

---

# Deliverable

A document, report, dataset, dashboard, or other output produced during a DAPM phase.

---

# Methodology

A structured approach that defines the phases, activities, and deliverables used to execute a data analytics project.
