# DAPM Principles

The following principles define the standards that every project following the Data Analytics Project Methodology (DAPM) should adhere to.

These principles ensure consistency, maintainability, and business relevance across different domains and projects.

---

# 1. Business First

Every project must begin by understanding the business problem before selecting technologies, designing solutions, or writing code.

The objective is to solve business problems, not technical problems.

---

# 2. Stakeholder-Centric Thinking

Every analysis should be performed from the perspective of a clearly identified stakeholder.

Business questions, metrics, KPIs, insights, and recommendations should support stakeholder decision-making.

---

# 3. Business Objectives Guide Data Exploration

Business questions define the direction of analysis.

The data should be explored only after understanding what decisions need to be supported.

---

# 4. Evidence-Driven Decision Making

Every observation, insight, and recommendation must be supported by evidence found in the data.

Assumptions should never be presented as facts.

---

# 5. Trust the Data Before Using the Data

Validation and cleaning must be completed before exploratory analysis begins.

Poor data quality leads to unreliable business conclusions.

---

# 6. Separate Facts from Interpretation

DAPM distinguishes between three levels of understanding.

- **Observations** describe what the data shows.
- **Business Insights** explain what those observations mean.
- **Recommendations** suggest what actions should be taken.

These should never be mixed.

---

# 7. One Phase, One Deliverable

Every phase must produce a unique deliverable.

If two phases produce the same outcome, the methodology should be simplified.

---

# 8. Technology Independence

DAPM is independent of programming languages, databases, visualization tools, and business intelligence platforms.

The methodology remains applicable whether the implementation uses spreadsheets, SQL, Python, Power BI, Tableau, or any other technology.

---

# 9. Continuous Documentation

Documentation should evolve throughout the project lifecycle.

Project decisions, assumptions, challenges, and lessons learned should be recorded as they occur.

---

# 10. Continuous Improvement

Every completed project should be used to evaluate and improve DAPM.

The methodology should evolve through practical experience rather than theoretical assumptions.
