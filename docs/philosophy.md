# DAPM Philosophy

## Why DAPM Exists

The Data Analytics Project Methodology (DAPM) was created to provide a practical, repeatable, and business-focused approach to solving analytics problems.

Many learning resources focus heavily on tools and implementation. DAPM instead begins with understanding the business, identifying stakeholder needs, and delivering actionable recommendations supported by evidence.

The methodology is designed to help analysts build solutions that answer business questions rather than simply produce technical outputs.

---

# Philosophy

DAPM is built on the following beliefs.

## 1. Business Before Technology

Technology is a tool, not the objective.

Understanding the business problem always comes before selecting tools such as SQL, Python, Power BI, or spreadsheets.

---

## 2. Stakeholders Drive the Analysis

Every analytics project exists because someone needs to make a decision.

The analyst's responsibility is to understand stakeholder needs before analyzing data.

---

## 3. Data Must Earn Trust

Insights are only valuable when they are based on reliable data.

Validation and cleaning are essential steps before any analysis begins.

---

## 4. Evidence Before Conclusions

Business conclusions should be supported by observations found in the data.

Recommendations should never be based on assumptions alone.

---

## 5. Observations Are Not Insights

EDA produces observations.

Business thinking transforms observations into insights.

Insights lead to recommendations.

Recommendations support business decisions.

---

## 6. Documentation Is Part of the Project

Documentation should evolve throughout the project rather than being written only after implementation.

A well-documented project communicates not only the final solution but also the reasoning behind important decisions.

---

## 7. Every Phase Must Produce Value

Each phase of DAPM should have a clear objective, a measurable outcome, and a unique deliverable.

If two phases produce the same outcome, the methodology should be simplified.

---

## 8. Continuous Improvement

DAPM is an evolving methodology.

Every completed project is an opportunity to evaluate, refine, and improve the framework for future use.

---

# Vision

DAPM is an evolving methodology that is continuously refined through real-world projects and practical experience.
